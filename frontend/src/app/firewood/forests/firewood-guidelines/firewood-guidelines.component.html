Firewood permit information

With a firewood permit, you can cut and collect firewood from the {{forest.forestName}}.

When to cut firewood

Firewood permits are available through the calendar year. However, firewood cutting is not allowed at certain times of year in parts of the forest.

See prohibited areas and restrictions for more information.

Cost

Firewood permits are $20.00 for 4 cords of wood. All permits are nonrefundable.

Buy a permit

You can buy firewood permits online or in-person at one of our district offices.

What kind of wood you can collect

All dead and down trees may be gathered. Live trees may NOT be cut.

Standing dead trees may be cut only if they are within 200 feet of a legal public road.

Where you can cut

Cutting and gather is only allowed on areas open to cutting and gathering on the firewood maps.

You can download a firemap below, or pick one up at one of our district offices.

Download a firewood cutting map

Prohibited areas and restrictions

  • No cutting or gathering of dead and/or down wood will occur within 200 feet of any lake, pond, stream, or river within 200 feet of any recreation area.
  • Live tree may NOT be cut.
  • Standing dead trees may be cut only if they are within 200 feet of a legal public road.
  • No gathering or harvesting of any dead trees identified by having pain marks at breast height and stump height or dead trees which have directional, trail, den tree, Animal Inn or land survey signs attached.

What to expect

Tagging your load

Before hauling wood out of the forest, you will need to fill out a load tag and attach it to the back of your vehicle. The load tag helps law enforcement see that you have a valid permit and have collected your wood legally.

You'll get your load tags after you purchase your permit. Each load tag is equal to 1/2 cord of wood.

How many tags will you need?

Filling out tags for online permits

Online permit buyers are emailed black and white load tags that you will need to print with your permit

To validate your tag, you will need to:

  • Fill in the day and the month that you are harvesting the wood with a black marker. For example, if you harvested on the 4th of July, you would fill in "4" for day and "7" for month.
  • Fold your tag on the dotted lines
  • Place each tag in a zip-top bag, and close the bag.
  • Nail your tag(s) to a piece of wood in the back of your load so that it is visible to cars behind you.

Filling out tags for in-person permits

You will be given colored load tags when you buy your permit from a district office or a registered vendor.

To validate your tag, you will need to:

  • Punch out the month
  • Punch out the date
  • Nail your tag(s) to a piece of wood in the back of your load so that it is visible to cars behind you.
