Equipment you'll need

Chainsaw operators must have a serviceable fire extinguisher (liquid 8 oz. or dry chemical 1 lb.) and shovel available at all times.

Chainsaws must be equipped with an approved spark arrestor screen.

All vehicles must be equipped with a baffled muffler and serviceable tailpipe.

A shovel and a bucket are mandatory when traveling through or on National Forest lands during fire season.

What to expect

Dead trees can be dangerous when they fall. Make sure you understand how to collect dead trees safely, using the right equipment and skills. 

Forest Service roads may have heavy logging traffic. Stay alert and give log truck and equipment plenty of space.

Park in areas so that traffic can get by safely and do not block gates.

The permit must be in your possession while harvesting and transporting products. Copies are not allowed and the permit is nontransferable.

Using your Firewood

Gathering of campwood is allowed without a permit as long as the wood is burned at your camp. A permit is required to transport any unused firewood home.

What to expect

The permit must be in your possession while cutting and transporting the firewood.

The person purchasing the permit (permittee) or a member of the permittee's immediate family must be present when cutting, gathering, and transporting firewood.

Stumps shall be cut flat and less than 12 inches above the ground.

Limbs, tops, and other debris will be removed from roads, ditches, streams, lakes and scattered to a depth of less than 24 inches high on the surrounding landscape.

Chainsaws must be in good repair and equipped with a spark arrester with a 0.023 screen.

The maximum length of wood removed must be less than 8 feet in length on logs that are 7 inches or larger in diameter. The 7-inch diameter is measured on the large end of the log.

Skidding of logs with any motorized vehicle is prohibited as multiple trips causes ruts, 2 tracks ruts and bare soil resulting in channeling water that erodes soil and causes compaction that further erodes soil.

Using your firewood
