Tagging your load

Before hauling wood out of the forest, you will need to fill out a load tag and attach it to the back of your vehicle. The load tag helps law enforcement see that you have a valid permit and have collected your wood legally.

You'll get your load tags after you purchase your permit. Each load tag is equal to 1/2 cord of wood.

Filling out tags for online permits

Online permit buyers are emailed black and white load tags that you will need to print with your permit

To validate your tag, you will need to:

  • Fill in the day and the month that you are harvesting the wood with a black marker. For example, if you harvested on the 4th of July, you would fill in "4" for day and "7" for month.
  • Fold your tag on the dotted lines
  • Place each tag in a zip-top bag, and close the bag.
  • Nail your tag(s) to a piece of wood in the back of your load so that it is visible to cars behind you.

Filling out tags for in-person permits

You will be given colored load tags when you buy your permit from a district office or a registered vendor.

To validate your tag, you will need to:

  • Punch out the month
  • Punch out the date
  • Nail your tag(s) to a piece of wood in the back of your load so that it is visible to cars behind you.
