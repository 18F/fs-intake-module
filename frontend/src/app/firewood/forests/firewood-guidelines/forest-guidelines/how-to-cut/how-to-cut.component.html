Equipment you'll need

Chainsaw operators must have a serviceable fire extinguisher (liquid 8 oz. or dry chemical 1 lb.) and shovel available at all times.

Chainsaws must be equipped with an approved spark arrestor screen.

All vehicles must be equipped with a baffled muffler and serviceable tailpipe.

A shovel and a bucket are mandatory when traveling through or on National Forest lands during fire season.

Park in areas so that traffic can get by safely and do not block gates.

Tagging your load

Before hauling wood out of the forest, you will need to fill out a load tag and attach it to the back of your vehicle. The load tag helps law enforcement see that you  have a valid permit and have collected your wood legally.

You'll get your load tags after you purchase your permit. Each load tag is equal to a 1/2 cord of wood.

How many tags do you need?

For each cord that you purchase, you will need 2 load tags. Ex. If you purchase 6 cords, you will need 12 load tags.

Filling out tags for online permits

Online firewood permit buyers are emailed black and white load tags that you will need to print with your permit.

To validate your tag, you'll need to:

  • Cut the paper with your two load tags in half to separate the tags.
  • Fill in the day and month that you're harvesting the wood with a black marker. For example, if you harvested on the 4th of July, you would fill in "7" for the month and "4" for the day.
  • Fold your tag on the dotted lines.
  • Place each tag in its own zip-top bag, and close the bag.
  • Nail your tag(s) to a piece of wood in the back of your load so that the side with the USDA logo is visible to cars behind you.
