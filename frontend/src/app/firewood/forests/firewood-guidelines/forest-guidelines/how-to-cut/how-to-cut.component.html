Load tags are required for permits bought online

If you get your permit online, you’ll need to tag your load by filling out and attaching a load tag to your firewood before you haul it out of the forest. You'll get your load tags after you purchase your permit.

How to tag your load

After you purchase your permit online, you’ll be emailed black and white load tags along with your permit. You will need to print your load tags and your permit and bring them with you when you go to the forest.

Fill out your tag(s) after you've loaded your wood, but before you start hauling it.

To fill out a tag, you'll need to:

  • Write in the day and the month that you get the wood with a black marker. For example, if you get wood on the 4th of July, you would fill in "4" for day and "7" for month.

To attach a tag, you'll need to:

  • Fold your tag on the dotted lines.
  • Place each tag in a zip-top bag, and close the bag.
  • Nail your tag(s) to a piece of wood in the back of your load so that it is visible to cars behind you.

How to tag your load if you bought your permit at a District office or from a vendor.

You’ll be given colored load tags when you buy your permit from a district office or a registered vendor.

Fill out and attach your tag(s) after you’ve loaded your wood, but before you start hauling it.

To fill out a tag, you'll need to:

  • Punch out the month
  • Punch out the date

To attach a tag, you'll need to:

  • Nail your tag to a piece of wood in the back of your load so that it’s visible to cars behind you.

How many tags you'll need

Load tags are in 1/2 cord increments. So, for example, if you're hauling 1 cord of wood, you'll need to fill in and attach 2 tags.
