What kind of wood you can collect

All dead and down trees may be gathered. Live trees may NOT be cut.

Standing dead trees may be cut unless are

  • Larch trees which may still be alive. Check the ground to see if needles have recently fallen. Check to see if the bark inside is tight. If it is, the tree is probably alive.
  • Cedar trees. This wood is unsuitable for firewood.
  • Pacific yew.
  • Wildlife trees. Standing and down dead trees provide a source of food, nests, perches, and protective cover for many birds and mammals in the forest. Please don't cut trees with paint marks or signs, broken tops, trunk holes or visible nests.

Where you can cut

Cutting and gather is only allowed on areas open to cutting and gathering on the firewood maps.

Prohibited areas and restrictions

  • No cutting or gathering of dead and/or down wood will occur within 200 feet of any lake, pond, stream, or river within 200 feet of any recreation area.
  • Live tree may NOT be cut.
  • Standing dead trees may be cut only if they are within 200 feet of a legal public road.
  • No gathering or harvesting of any dead trees identified by having pain marks at breast height and stump height or dead trees which have directional, trail, den tree, Animal Inn or land survey signs attached.

What kind of wood you can collect

All dead and down trees may be gathered. Live trees may NOT be cut.

Where you can gather

When searching for firewood at the Chattahoochee-Oconee National Forest, be sure to use designated motorized roads and to be aware of seasonal road closures.

Maps and travel publications are available online and at ranger district offices.

Prohibited areas and restrictions

  • No gathering of dead and/or down wood will occur within 150 feet of any running stream, pond, lake or marshy or wet area unless otherwise designated by the agency on whose land you wish to harvest firewood.
  • Live trees may NOT be cut.
  • Privately owned lands within the National Forests are often marked by signs and fences. Look at your Forest map to be sure you are not cutting on private land.
  • No gathering any dead trees identified by having paint marks. signs or tags.
  • No gathering in all developed recreation areas, campgrounds, and wild and scenic river corridors.
  • No gathering within active timber sale units or other areas signed or shown on the firewood gathering area map as being closed to firewood gathering.

How to gather your firewood

How much wood can be gathered? 4 cords/$20 minimum purchase

Firewood is commonly measured in cords. A standard cord is the amount of lightly stacked wood 4 feet wide by 4 feet high by 8 feet long.

Always wear proper clothing with PPE and safely operate equipment.
